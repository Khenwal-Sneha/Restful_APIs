Introduction to EE

The teaching, learning and assessment strategies of engineering education need to evolve to meet the need of the 21st-century engineer. The traditional teaching method like lecturing with chalk and talk, question - answering and problem-solving have been in place for a long time and it shows inabilities to help the student to learn. Conversely, active learning methods are more effective than conventional teaching. In today’s undergraduate engineering curricula of any discipline contain project-based courses, either as a mini-projector, major or capstone project at the final year level. These projects are meant to address certain graduate attributes (GAs) defined by ABET. Attributes like teamwork, creativity, application of concepts, design, communication, project management, and entrepreneurship skills are touched upon through projects. Notably, the product design and development skill are less emphasized in these assignments, though student teams come with certain models. It lacks in certain aspects like innovativeness, reliability, feasibility, and economics. So, it is important to consider all the aspects while designing and developing a product to be a commercial product. In this paper, an introductory course at entry-level of undergraduate studies is introduced for all disciplines of engineering, which comprises of learning through activities and doing by hands through experimentation named as “Engineering Exploration of Design Project”. The activity-based teaching-learning is carried out in special designed classroom known as ‘Learning Studio’ and a laboratory kind of place for product development works known as thinkering laboratory, which consists of desired mechanical tools, electronics items like- sensors, actuators, etc., and electrical appliances which are necessary for any product development.
The engagement of first-year engineering students in the learning process is crucial in terms of excitement, retention, and satisfaction. The engagement of the students in the learning process often involves instructional modification, specifically the introduction of active learning tools. The active learning tool also leads to the reduction of failure rate by reforming the exam pattern as compared to the traditional methods of teaching, learning, and assessment. The activity-based learning method is designed to provide the conceptual understanding through the collaborative engagement of the student in hands-on activities which yield immediate feedback through discussion with peers and/or instructors.

Course Objectives

1. To learn skills associated with the tools and inventory associated with the Engineering Exploration and IDEALab.
2. To learn concepts of innovation including design thinking and go through the concept of a typical multidisciplinary group project.
3. To learn useful mechanical and electronic fabrication processes in multidisciplinary way.
4. To learn necessary skills to build prototypes of useful and standalone system/project with enclosures.
5. To learn important skills to create print and electronic documentation for the system/project.

Course Content

Unit 1: Introduction to Engineering, Engineering Study and Ethics ( 04 hours)
Unit 2: Innovation Cycle and Engineering Design (04 hours)
Unit 3: Basic components of a typical Multidisciplinary Project (16 hours)
Unit 4: Familiarization to Tools, Basic measurement instruments and equip-ment (04 hours)
Unit 5: Circuit prototyping, Introduction to 2D/3D design and PCBdesign (06 hours)
Unit 6: 3D printing and prototyping, Mechanical cutting and engraving (06 hours)
Unit 7: Introduction to Project Management (06 hours)
Unit 8: Course project (14 hours)

Laboratory Activities

1. Simple innovative activities/experiments.
2. Simple mechanisms using Software simulators.
3. Use of measuring tools and equipment.
4. Electronics Circuit Design using simulators and breadboards.
5. Embedded programming using Arduino and/or Raspberry Pi including interfacing with typical sensors/transducers and actuators/LED/Display/etc.
6. Schematic and PCB layout design of a suitable circuit, fabrication and testing of the circuit.
7. 2D prole cutting of press t box/casing in acrylic/cardboard/MDF using laser cutter & engraver.
8. 3D scanning of computer mouse geometry surface and 3D printing of scanned geometry using FDMor SLAprinter.
9. Machining of 3D geometry on soft material such as soft wood or modelling wax.
10. Use of project management tools.
11. Documentation of project.
12. Course project (Design and implementation of a project involving embedded hardware, software and machined or 3D printed enclosure)

Course Outcomes

At the end of the course student will be able to
CO1. Know the concepts of engineering, engineering ethics and concerns of modern-day engineers performing the role of as a problem solver by understanding innovation cycle, design thinking, engineering design, multidisciplinary group project, and in-tellectual property.
CO2. Understand basic tools and instruments necessary to implement a typical multi-disciplinary project.
CO3. Understand concepts of an electronic project design using circuit building blocks like sensors, transducers, actuators, LEDs, Displays, and typical processors like Arduino.
CO4. Understand and perform 2D/3D design, 2D/3D CNC machining, 3D printing, laser cutting and engraving, and PCBdesigning.
CO5. Understand basics of engineering project management skills and implement a typical multidisciplinary group project with its documentation.
