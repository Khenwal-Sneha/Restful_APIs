Team 5

Project Name

Metal Detector

Abstract

The metal detector bot operates based on the principle of electromagnetic induction, where a metal object alters the electromagnetic field generated by the sensor's search coil. This disturbance is detected by the sensor and amplified through an amplifier circuit connected to an Arduino board. The Arduino processes the amplified signal, comparing it to a predefined threshold value to determine metal presence. When the signal exceeds this threshold, indicating metal detection, the Arduino triggers an output signal, activating a buzzer or LED for user notification. Sensitivity can be adjusted through calibration, allowing for fine-tuning of the detection range and minimizing false positives. Users interact with the metal detector by passing metal objects near the sensor, receiving immediate feedback through the activated buzzer or LED. In essence, the metal detector bot effectively detects metal objects by sensing changes in the electromagnetic field and promptly alerting users to their presence.

Ultrasonic Radar Sensor

Abstract

Radar systems are crucial in various applications including surveillance, navigation, and object detection. However, traditional radar systems often require sophisticated hardware and are costly to implement. This paper proposes a cost-effective radar system utilizing Arduino microcontrollers, which offer a versatile and accessible platform for rapid prototyping and deployment. The proposed radar system comprises an ultrasonic sensor and a servo motor mounted on a rotating platform controlled by an Arduino board. The ultrasonic sensor emits high-frequency sound waves, which bounce off objects in the environment and are detected upon their return. By analyzing the time delay between transmission and reception of these waves, the distance to the objects can be accurately calculated.
